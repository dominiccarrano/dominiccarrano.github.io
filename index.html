Academia

Teaching

My time at school is invovles a lot of teaching, and I wouldn't have it any other way. I'm involved in teaching in two capacities:
  1. I'm head TA for EE 120, UC Berkeley's Signals and Systems class. EE 120 is typically the first upper division electrical engineering class students take. It covers linear system theory, Fourier analysis, and sampling theory. I've served on the course staff for two semesters now, one as a TA (Fall 2018) and one as head TA (Spring 2019). One of the best things about being a TA is how much impact I'm able to have on developing the course. This semester, as head TA, I'm leading a several efforts to develop the class:
    • iPython Labs.
    • Discussion Worksheets.
    • A Class Website.
  2. I'm also the EE 16A Co-Coordinator for Computer Science Mentors (CSM). CSM is a club at UC Berkeley that offers auxiliary tutoring sections of 4-6 students for all of Berkeley's lower division EECS courses, helping students have a more personalized learning experience in classes that typically have enrollments of 1000+ students each semester. I've been involved in CSM's EE 16A activities for three semesters now, teaching EE 16A sections on topics including linear algebra, circuit theory, and optimization, in addition to helping manage and lead other mentors through some of my more recent roles as Co-Coordinator (Spring 2019) and Senior Mentor (Fall 2018).
