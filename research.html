Research

I'm broadly interested in the information systems and sciences, including signal processing, information and coding theory, optimization, and machine learning, as well as distributed computing. I'm especially interested in applying techniques from these areas to solving engineering problems.

I'm very fortunate to be working as an undergraduate research assistant in the BASiCS Lab with Professor Kannan Ramchandran and Vipul Gupta, where we're studying problems related to accelerating cloud computing tasks by leveraging ideas from information and coding theory.

In summers 2018 and 2019, I worked at the National Ignition Facility (NIF) at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory, developing new signal processing methods for enhancing laser pulse measurements for NIF diagnostic systems. I presented some of the work done at SPIE LASE in February 2019 in San Francisco, which you can find below.
